Games to Prevent Alzheimer's Disease

Word Building

Word building games, such as Scrabble or word search puzzles, can help improve language skills, memory, and cognitive function. These games stimulate the brain and provide a fun way to prevent Alzheimer's disease.

Jigsaw Puzzle

Jigsaw puzzles are excellent for keeping the mind active and engaged, as they require visual-spatial reasoning and memory. Completing jigsaw puzzles can help maintain cognitive function and reduce the risk of developing Alzheimer's.

Chess

Chess is a strategic board game that challenges the brain, promoting critical thinking, problem-solving, and memory skills, which can help prevent Alzheimer's disease. Regularly playing chess can stimulate the brain and improve cognitive function.

Jenga

Jenga is a game where players take turns removing blocks from a tower and balancing them on top, testing their dexterity and strategy. Playing Jenga can improve fine motor skills, concentration, and decision-making abilities.
