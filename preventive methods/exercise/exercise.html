Exercise Plan for Alzheimer's Disease

Brisk Walking

Brisk walking is a beneficial exercise for individuals with Alzheimer's disease, as it helps improve cardiovascular health, mood, and overall well-being. Aim for at least 30 minutes of brisk walking most days of the week, but start at a level that is comfortable for the individual.

Arm Stretches

Arm stretches can help improve flexibility and range of motion, which is important for daily activities. These stretches should be done gently and without causing any pain. Hold each stretch for about 15-30 seconds and repeat a few times.

Heel Rises

Heel rises are a simple yet effective exercise for strengthening the calf muscles, which can help with balance and stability. Stand with feet hip-width apart, rise up onto the balls of the feet, then lower back down. Repeat 10-15 times, gradually increasing as strength improves.

Yoga

Yoga incorporates physical postures, breathing exercises, and meditation, benefiting individuals with Alzheimer's by enhancing flexibility, strength, and balance. Gentle yoga poses, practiced with awareness and modified as necessary, can promote relaxation and overall well-being.
